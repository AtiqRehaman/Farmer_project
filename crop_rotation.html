Crop Rotation Process

Step 1: Plan the Rotation Cycle

Crop rotation involves planning the sequence of crops to be grown in a specific field over several seasons. This helps maintain soil fertility and reduce pest buildup.

Step 2: Divide the Field into Sections

Divide the field into sections and assign different crops to each section. Rotate crops annually to ensure balanced nutrient usage.

Step 3: Rotate Legumes and Grains

Alternate legumes (e.g., beans, peas) with grains (e.g., wheat, corn) to replenish nitrogen in the soil and improve its structure.

Step 4: Monitor Soil Health

Regularly test the soil to monitor nutrient levels and pH. Adjust the rotation plan based on soil health and crop performance.

Step 5: Incorporate Cover Crops

Use cover crops (e.g., clover, rye) during off-seasons to prevent soil erosion, suppress weeds, and improve soil fertility.
